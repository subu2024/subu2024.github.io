A Four-Step Framework for Harnessing AI

published Date: Sep 2023 |  5 minutes read

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming industries by solving complex problems and automating tasks. In business, AI presents an immense opportunity, especially in areas like Human Resources (HR). This article presents a four-step framework to harness AI's potential, using employee attrition in HR as a case study.

Step 1: Identify the Appropriate AI Process or Intelligence

Start by defining the specific AI process needed. This step is foundational and requires careful consideration. Identify a problem AI can solve effectively, set performance metrics, and define the intelligence's scope.

In HR, addressing attrition might involve using predictive analytics to forecast which employees are likely to leave. Performance metrics could include predicting attrition "days in advance" with high accuracy. This allows HR to take timely action, reducing turnover rates.

Step 2: Identify the Relevant Business Process

Next, pinpoint the business process that will benefit most from AI. Consider strategic and operational factors. Three business models are key: being the best product player, a full customer solutions provider, or focusing on network externalities.

In the attrition case, the relevant business process is talent management and employee retention. AI integrates here strategically, aiding in talent growth, succession planning, and employee morale. Combining internal corporate data with anonymized external data creates meaningful benchmarks for accurate predictions.

Step 3: Select the Appropriate AI Algorithm

Once the AI process and business process are defined, choose the right AI algorithm. This involves intellectual property approaches and data strategies. Data is crucial for AI, and leveraging external data sources is essential.

Machine learning algorithms like logistic regression, decision trees, or neural networks can analyze employee data to predict attrition risks. For example, logistic regression could identify patterns in employee behavior that indicate a high risk of leaving.

Step 4: Implement the Solution: Code, Training, and Integration

Finally, bring the AI solution to life. Address talent acquisition, development methodologies, cloud platform choices, data acquisition, and cost analysis. Tackling "AI Cancers" like bias and lack of generalization is also critical.

Bias can arise from skewed training data, leading to unfair predictions. Lack of generalization means the AI struggles to adapt to new situations. For the attrition problem, HR professionals need to implement the AI solution effectively, writing code, preprocessing data, and continually training the algorithm.

Proactive measures based on AI predictions, such as personalized coaching or salary adjustments, can help retain employees. For instance, if the AI predicts that a top performer is likely to leave, HR can offer incentives to retain them.

In conclusion, this structured AI framework involves critical decisions across four stages. By selecting the right AI process, identifying the appropriate business process, choosing the correct algorithm, and implementing the solution, organizations can achieve automation, efficiency, and innovation. This approach can significantly improve HR practices, reducing attrition and enhancing employee satisfaction.
