Time Value of Money

published Date: Jun 2024 |  3 minutes read



Understanding the Time Value of Money (TVM) is crucial for making informed financial decisions. TVM is the idea that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its earning potential. Let’s break this down with a simple example.

Example: The $100 Scenario

Imagine you have $100 today. You can either spend it or invest it. If you invest it at a 5% annual interest rate, in one year, you’ll have $105. This growth shows that $100 today is more valuable than $100 a year from now.

Key Concepts of TVM:

  1. Present Value (PV): The current value of a future sum of money. For instance, if you need $105 in one year and the interest rate is 5%, the present value is $100.

  2. Future Value (FV): The value of a current sum of money at a specific time in the future. Using the same example, the future value of $100 today is $105 in one year with 5% interest.

  3. Interest Rate: The percentage at which your money grows annually. A higher rate means more future value.

  4. Time Period: The duration your money is invested or borrowed. The longer the time, the higher the future value due to compounding interest.

Why TVM Matters:

  • Investing: Helps you understand how much your investments will grow over time.
  • Loans: Assists in evaluating the true cost of loans and mortgages.
  • Retirement Planning: Guides you in estimating how much you need to save for the future.

Quick Calculation:

If you want to find out how much $1,000 invested at 6% interest will be worth in 3 years, use the formula: [ FV = PV \ (1 + r)^n ] Where: - ( PV = 1000 ) - ( r = 0.06 ) - ( n = 3 )

[ FV = 1000 \ (1 + 0.06)^3 = 1000 \ 1.191016 = 1191.02 ]

So, $1,000 today will be worth $1,191.02 in 3 years.

Understanding TVM helps you make smarter financial decisions by appreciating the value of money over time.
