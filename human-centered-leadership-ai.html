Human-Centered Leadership in the AI Era

published Date: Feb 2024 |  5 minutes read

In our new world of AI, three core leadership skills are essential for those aiming to excel. While AI boosts productivity, the essence of the workplace remains human connection. Three people-centric leadership skills stand out—skills that AI won't replace anytime soon. Leading change, nurturing people development, and utilizing strategic thinking are areas where leaders shine, offering what AI cannot—emotional intelligence, genuine connection, and visionary planning.

Leading Change Effectively

Change is a constant in today’s workplaces. AI struggles to grasp human emotions and reactions to change, but leaders can connect with people on an emotional level, understanding their perspectives and concerns. This emotional intelligence is crucial for leading through change. By focusing on empathy and understanding, leaders create a supportive environment, acknowledging that change involves people, not just processes. This approach fosters resilience, agility, and a shared commitment to growth.

Example: Incorporate change into your company culture by discussing it in interviews, one-on-one meetings, onboarding, and team meetings. Normalize change with questions like: - "Can you describe a time when you had to adapt to a significant change at work? How did you manage the transition?” - "How do you stay productive and focused when faced with continuous change or uncertainty in the workplace?”

By making change a regular topic, you prepare your team to handle it smoothly, fostering a culture that views change as an opportunity rather than a threat.

Nurturing People Development

AI lacks the human touch that makes leaders excel at developing team capacity. Strategic thinking, genuine care, and the ability to form real connections are where AI falls short, but great leaders thrive. Empower your people to see every workday as an opportunity for growth. Use your uniquely human strategic thinking skills to chart your team's future, considering who's next in line and the skills needed to achieve your goals. People development is closely tied to engagement and retention, both crucial for organizational success.

Example: Set aside time regularly to understand your team members’ motivations, strengths, and goals. Establish a development chat cadence, perhaps quarterly or twice a year, and follow up in regular one-on-ones to track skill progress. Ongoing development discussions create opportunities and ensure your team feels like they have them, which is vital for their growth and retention.

Utilizing Strategic Thinking

AI excels at making fast decisions using data and spotting patterns, but leaders excel in strategic thinking. Unlike AI, humans can think logically and plan for the future, setting value-based goals, foreseeing potential problems, steering clear of unexpected issues, and bringing in the right people at the right times. This human skill is crucial for innovation, as AI relies solely on past information and lacks the lens of experience.

Example: While AI can analyze market trends and customer data to suggest potential strategies, a human leader can evaluate these strategies in the context of the company’s long-term vision and values. They can also consider external factors such as market sentiment and regulatory changes, which AI might overlook. By combining AI-driven insights with human judgment, leaders can create robust strategies that are both data-informed and aligned with the company’s mission.
